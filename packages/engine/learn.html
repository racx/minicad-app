MINICAD — watch & learn

Eight little movies, on repeat. Watch each one twice, then copy it in the app. That's the whole lesson.

The only things to memorize:
gap in front of a line → EXTEND  ·  extra bits past a crossing → TRIM  ·  "just make a corner" → FILLET
blue square → drag it to reshape  ·  parallel copy → OFFSET  ·  make a room longer → STRETCH

TRIM — cut off the extra legs

Two lines cross. The yellow cursor clicks the pieces that shouldn't exist — and they're gone. What's left is a corner.

type TR Enter Enter  →  click each piece you want GONE  →  Enter

EXTEND — grow a line until it lands

A line hangs in the air with a gap below it. Click near its dangling tip, and it grows until it touches the other line.

type EX Enter Enter  →  click near the TIP that should grow  →  Enter

FILLET, radius 0 — "make these two meet"

One line overshoots, the other is too short. Click each line on the side you want to KEEP — fillet trims one and stretches the other into a perfect corner. One command, no thinking.

type F Enter Enter  →  click line 1 (keep side)  →  click line 2 (keep side)

FILLET with a radius — the rounded corner

Same clicks, but with a radius set: the corner comes out rounded, like a coffee-table edge.

type F Enter then 40 Enter  →  click line 1  →  click line 2

GRIPS — reshape by dragging the blue squares

Click an object to select it and little blue squares appear. Drag an end square and the line stretches — and it magnetically snaps onto other objects, so connecting walls is effortless.

click an object  →  drag a blue square  ·  Esc mid-drag = abort  ·  U = undo

OFFSET — parallel copy (wall thickness in one move)

One rectangle is a room. Offset it inward by the wall thickness and you have both faces of the wall instantly.

type O Enter then 12 Enter  →  click the shape  →  click the INSIDE

MIRROR — the other half, for free

Draw half of anything symmetric, then flip a copy across a line. Enter at the end keeps the original.

select things  →  MI Enter  →  click 2 points of the mirror line  →  Enter

STRETCH — make the room longer without redrawing

The green right-to-left box chooses which corners move. Everything inside the box shifts; everything outside stays nailed down.

type S Enter  →  drag a box right→left over the end  →  Enter  →  click from  →  click to

PRINT TO SCALE — paper that measures true

No movie needed — it's a dialog. The idea: at 1:50, every 50 cm of your drawing becomes exactly 1 cm of paper, so a builder can measure your plan with a ruler. The one rule: in the browser's print dialog, keep scale at 100% — never "fit to page".

type UNITS Enter cm Enter  →  ⌘P  →  paper + 1:50 + pick window  →  Print…  ·  first time: press Test page and measure the 100 mm bar

Now open the app in the other tab and copy the movie you just watched. If it goes wrong: U + Enter, breathe, try again.
